Solar resource variability#

Introduction#

The sunlight available for collection by a PV system varies across several timescales: hourly (due to clouds), daily (due to day and night), seasonally (due to Earth’s orbit and tilt), and annually (due to long-term change in Earth’s atmosphere). The amount of sunlight available in a given location is called “solar resource”.

Different locations receive different amounts of solar resource. For example, sunny lower-latitude locations like Albuquerque receive about twice as much solar resource as cloudy higher-latitude locations like Seattle do.
